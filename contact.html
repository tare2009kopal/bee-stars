What happens during a weekly session?+
Each session opens with an in-depth lesson on a focused topic — diacritics, etymology, phonetics, or competition strategy — followed by interactive practice including live spelling rounds, word games, and activities. Sessions are structured to be engaging and educational, building skills that carry over into real competition.
How long is the program?+
Bee Stars runs for 10 weeks, with one 30-minute session per week on Sundays from 5:30–6:00 PM PST. The program takes place annually from November through January.
Where are sessions held?+
All sessions are held online via Zoom. After registering, you'll receive a Zoom link and any relevant materials before the first class. No special setup is required.
Is there a cost to participate?+
Bee Stars is completely free — no tuition, no materials fee, no hidden costs. It is a community service program, and that will never change.
Who can join?+
The program is designed for students in Grades K–7, but all motivated learners are welcome regardless of grade level or prior spelling bee experience. There are no prerequisites — students at all levels benefit from the foundational skills we cover.
Do students need prior spelling bee experience?+
Not at all. Bee Stars is designed to bring any student up to speed, whether they've never been in a spelling bee or are preparing for their fifth. The curriculum builds progressively across the 10-week session.
When does the next cohort start?+
The annual program starts in early November and runs through January. Register using the form below and you'll be notified of the exact start date and Zoom details as the session approaches.
