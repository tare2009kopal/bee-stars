About Bee Stars

Taught by a speller
who's been there.

Bee Stars is a free community spelling tutoring program founded by Kopal Tare, a junior at Dublin High School and a competitive speller with placements at the school, district, county, and state levels.

Through Bee Stars, Kopal brings firsthand competition experience directly to young students — offering structured lessons in the same strategies that shaped her own journey through spelling bees.

Sessions are held weekly on Sundays, 5:30–6:00 PM PST via Zoom, and run from November through January each year.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

What happens during a weekly session? +
Each session opens with an in-depth lesson on a core topic — such as diacritics, etymology, or phonetics — followed by interactive practice including spelling rounds, word games, and exercises. Sessions are structured to be both rigorous and engaging, so students build real skills while enjoying the process.
How long is the program? +
The program runs for 10 weeks, with one 30-minute session per week on Sundays from 5:30–6:00 PM PST. It takes place annually from November through January.
Where are sessions held? +
All sessions are conducted online via Zoom. Login details are sent out after you register — no setup or downloads required on your end.
Is there a cost to participate? +
Bee Stars is entirely free of charge — always. No hidden fees, no materials cost, no subscription. It is a community service program.
Who can join? +
The program is designed for students in Grades K–7, though all motivated learners are welcome regardless of grade level or prior spelling bee experience. There are no prerequisites.
